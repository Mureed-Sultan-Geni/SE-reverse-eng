Our pragmatic-first approach to a new framework

SetJS has been our main recipe for building SPAs and JS heavy web projects for more than 6 years. Before we created SetJS, we used React.js in a few small and medium size projects. However, we realised the heavy abstractions, which might work for really large scale projects, were more of a burden than a boon for our small team.

SetJS did not reach its current form immediately though, it took us years to perfect it to our needs. We decided on major principles, then refined our code to better achieve those principles over time.

Why another framework?
Minimal learning curve

Onboarding new developers is hard. We did not want anything that requires months of training. Also, if for some reason we decided to migrate to a new framework, the existing skills would be lost.

Stability

We hated how fast the framework world changed. Old being replaced by new so fast you lose track of what is in fashion today. We wanted a stable solution that does not require constant updating.

Real world battle-tested

SetJS has been production ready for many years now. It worked so well for our small team that we decided to share it with the world.

Simplicity

Simplicity and a thinner layer of abstractions than has become the norm. We found no reason why the frameworks were becoming so abstract. Abstractions have their place, but too many do more harm than good, especially for smaller projects.
